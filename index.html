Hoyeon Yu

Co-Founder & Robotics Engineer | Floatic

Hoyeon Yu is a Co-Founder and Robotics Engineer at Floatic, a logistics robotics startup. He leads the Embedded Engineering team at Floatic.

His work explores the intersection of robotics technology and society, with a particular interest in Human-Robot Interaction, Control Engineering and Systems Engineering. He graduated from Hanyang University ERICA with a B.S. in Robotics in 2021 and interned at NAVER LABS as a Robotics Engineer in 2020.

Academic Experience

The development of a Social Robot Accessible to the Deaf

Bada is a social robot that can interact with individuals with the deaf. It resembles the appearance of a robotic vacuum cleaner and its signaling of abnormal circumstances at home was modeled after the behavior of hearing dogs. Bada effectively reduce the loss of information during delivery by relaying messages in various ways including web service, text messages, visual representation, and haptic interface. We have developed Bada's interaction process through several tests. Its behavior, interface, and interaction model would fairly contribute to the robotic accessibility technology.

A Gait Estimation Algorithm for Handrail Mountable Walking Aid Handles for the Elderly

Hoyeon Yu worked as an undergraduate researcher at AIRO Lab from September 2019 to August 2020 under the supervision of Prof. Min-Sung Kang. He independently researched a gait estimation algorithm for handrail mountable walking aid handles for the elderly in the lab.

The object of reaserch is to create algorithm that recognition elderly person’s gait pattern and speed. This research is sponsored by National Research Foundation of Korea.

A person’s joints information of leg was extracted by a mobile robot with RGB-D camera and OpenPose(Skeleton tracking algorithm). After learning the data through LSTM in order to estimate the gait pattern and speed, the model was installed on a mobile robot that follows the person who walks next to the machine.
